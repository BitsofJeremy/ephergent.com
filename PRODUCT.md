# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Sci-fi explorers: fans of solarpunk, biopunk, steampunk fiction who enjoy rich world-building, audio drama enthusiasts who want to explore the Ephergent universe beyond the episodes. They discover the site through the podcast, SEO, or word of mouth and arrive curious about the terminal aesthetic.

## Product Purpose

Ephergent.com is the living broadcast log and world companion for The Ephergent Signal podcast. It lets fans (and future fans) explore lore entries, navigate crew manifests, replay transmissions, and play embedded games. Success means a visitor feels the weight of the universe before they've heard a single episode, and a returning fan finds something new each visit.

## Brand Personality

Voice: warm oddball. The universe is vast and strange, but the crew is small and kind. Think: Clive stapling the same things for ninety years, A1 navigating the dark between stars with coffee, Meatball tasting Thursday on the stars. Tone is earnest without being saccharine, specific without being cold.

Three-word personality: **Warm, quirky, character-driven**.

## Anti-references

What this should NOT look like:
- Generic sci-fi dark-blue-neon aesthetic (the category reflex)
- Corporate podcast website with a player and social links
- AI-generated content feel: gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics, identical card grids
- Cold, clinical, or impersonal interfaces

The terminal aesthetic is specific to this project: CRT phosphor amber/cyan, not generic "hacker green." The world-building is earnest and characterful, not ironic or self-aware.

## Design Principles

1. **The station feels inhabited.** Every design choice should reinforce that a crew lives here. Personalities show through UI copy, status messages, and error states.

2. **Atmosphere before clarity.** Users should feel the universe before they understand it. Dense information architecture is acceptable if it rewards exploration.

3. **Specificity is charm.** Named colors, named crew roles, named frequencies. No generic "navigation" when it could be "STATION TERMINAL." No "submit" when it could be "BROADCAST SIGNAL."

4. **Discovery over completeness.** The site should reward return visits. A new visitor sees the surface; a returning fan finds depth. Not everything needs to be on one page.

5. **Earn the weird.** The strangest elements (meatball's haikus, the Cybernetic Dinosaur Banking Consortium) are the most memorable. Treat them as craft, not novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all interactive elements and text contrast
- Screen reader support for lore entries, episode metadata, and navigation
- Keyboard navigation for power users
- Reduced motion respected (prefers-reduced-motion already in CSS)
- No time-limited interactions
- Alt text on all character portraits and visual assets
