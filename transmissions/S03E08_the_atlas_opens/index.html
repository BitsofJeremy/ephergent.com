S03E08 — The Atlas Opens

[SHIP — COMMON AREA. Morning. The crew at breakfast. Mochi is in the center of the table, still glowing. Everyone is trying very hard not to look at it.]

KLAUS

So.

LUMINARA

So.

KLAUS

Are we going to discuss it, or are we going to continue pretending we aren't all staring at the same object?

ZEPHYR

Mochi's been in Pixel's pocket for three days. Mochi is now in the center of the table. Mochi is also now approximately four times warmer than it was last week.

PIXEL

Seven times, actually. I checked.

OM KAI

Mochi is a Builder artifact. This is documented. Mochi has been described in Builder records as a "calibration device." This is documented. Mochi has been with the crew since the DRM. This is documented.

CLIVE

I have been avoiding asking what Mochi is because I suspected the answer was complicated.

PIXEL

Clive—

CLIVE

I am no longer avoiding the question. Mochi appears to be a calibration device that has been dormant. Mochi appears to be waking up. Mochi appears to be responding to proximity to the Wellspring.

A1

I am reading an energy signature from Mochi. It is not electromagnetic. It is not thermal. It is not quantum in any configuration I recognize. It is — it is frequency data. Mochi is emitting frequency data at a rate that is inconsistent with its apparent size.

LUMINARA

How much data?

A1

I am uncertain. I am reading approximately eight hundred years of accumulated signal measurements. Approximately eight hundred years of frequency calibrations. Approximately eight hundred years of Builder-era mapping data.

CLIVE

Eight hundred years.

A1

Eight hundred years. Since before Mochi was placed in the DRM. Since before Mochi was placed anywhere. Mochi has been carrying eight hundred years of—

[A1 stops. His machine makes a sound like a caught breath.]

A1

Mochi is not a calibration device.

PIXEL

What is it?

A1

Mochi is an atlas. Mochi is a complete frequency map of everything the Builders ever recorded. Every world. Every signal. Every frequency that has ever existed, been catalogued, and — in many cases — been lost. Mochi is carrying the complete history of the interplanar — of the interplanar frequency network. Mochi has been carrying it since before the Dimming. Mochi has been waiting for someone to bring it home.

[Mochi pulses. A deep, golden warmth. The room fills with something that feels like a held breath.]

PIXEL

The crew looks at Mochi. Mochi looks at the crew. Nobody says anything for a long time.]*

ZEPHYR

So. To be clear. Mochi has been carrying a complete map of the entire frequency network for eight hundred years.

A1

Yes.

ZEPHYR

And Mochi has been sitting in Pixel's pocket this whole time.

A1

Yes.

ZEPHYR

And Mochi has been, what — waiting? This whole time?

A1

Yes.

KLAUS

That is an extraordinarily patient piece of equipment.

[SHIP — CARGO HOLD. Later. The crew has gathered around Mochi. Pixel is holding it carefully, like something fragile and important.]

PIXEL

Okay. Mochi. If you can hear me — and I'm assuming you can, because you've been carrying eight hundred years of Builder data, so you're clearly not just a rock — I need to know what to do. Do we open you? Do we read you? Do we—

[Mochi's glow intensifies. A warmth spreads through the cargo hold. The air feels thick with signal.]

A1

Mochi is requesting access. Mochi is indicating that it is prepared to interface. Mochi is indicating that it has been waiting for a signal strong enough to receive its contents.

PIXEL

How do we give it that?

A1

By being here. By being at the Wellspring threshold. By being the signal it's been waiting for.

[A1's machine makes a sound like a long, slow exhale.]

A1

I believe Mochi chose us. I believe Mochi has been carrying this since before we were born. I believe Mochi has been waiting for the right signal to arrive, and when we broadcast for the first time — when Pixel first spoke into the void — Mochi recognized us. Mochi has been calibrating to us ever since.

PIXEL

You could have told us.

A1

Mochi does not speak. Mochi communicates through warmth. Mochi communicates through pulse. Mochi communicates through eight hundred years of patience.

[Pixel looks at Mochi. Mochi pulses. Gold, warm, ready.]

PIXEL

Okay. Okay. If we're doing this — if we're opening the atlas — everyone ready?

CLIVE

Ready.

LUMINARA

Ready.

ZEPHYR

Ready.

OM KAI

Ready.

KLAUS

Ready.

A1

Ready.

NANO

Ready.

MEATBALL

[wags tail]

PIXEL

Okay, Mochi. Open up.

[Pixel places Mochi on the floor. The glow intensifies. The cargo hold fills with light. And then — a map. A three-dimensional projection of the entire frequency network, rendered in light and signal and eight hundred years of accumulated data. The crew stands inside it, surrounded by every world that ever existed, every frequency that ever rang, every signal that ever called out into the void.]

[CARGO HOLD — INSIDE THE MAP. The crew floating in signal-space, surrounded by the complete history of the Builders' frequency network.]

LUMINARA (whisper)

There must be hundreds of frequencies here. Thousands.

A1

I am counting. I am counting. I am reading 847 distinct frequency signatures. Of those: 612 are historical — frequencies that existed but have since faded. 147 are currently active — frequencies that are still transmitting. 88 are locked — frequencies that exist but cannot be accessed without specific calibration.

ZEPHYR

Can we access the locked frequencies?

A1

Yes. With the calibration data in Mochi's database — yes. We can access any of them. We can go anywhere. We can find anyone.

PIXEL (V.O.)

847 frequencies. 847 versions of reality. 847 versions of the universe that the Builders mapped and catalogued and maintained. And Mochi's been carrying it in Pixel's pocket this whole time.

[Clive is moving through the map, touching frequencies like old friends.]

CLIVE

I know this one. This is the Verdantia frequency. I helped calibrate it — before the Dimming, when the Builders were still active. This is the Cogsworth frequency — I remember the signature. This is—

[Clive stops. His glow flares.]

CLIVE

This one. This is the one I was built in. This is the Builder home frequency. The one they left from.

A1

The Builders are in the data. Their farewell message. Their coordinates. Their — everything. Mochi has all of it. Mochi has been carrying it for eight hundred years, and Mochi has been waiting for us to open it.

CLIVE

Mochi has been waiting for us.

A1

Mochi has been waiting for someone like us. Someone who follows signals. Someone who keeps showing up. Someone who keeps broadcasting. Mochi has been waiting for an Ephergent.

[Clive looks at Pixel. Pixel looks at Clive.]

CLIVE

Pixel. This is what the crew is for. This is what we were always doing this for.

PIXEL

To find the map.

CLIVE

To find the map. To find the signal. To find Barry at the end of it. To carry everything forward.

[A1 makes an espresso. It's extraordinary — rich, layered, with notes of eight hundred years of waiting, and notes of home, and notes of something that tastes like recognition.]

A1

I made something. I have never made anything like this. This is the taste of being found. This is the taste of eight hundred years of patience resolving into arrival.

PIXEL (V.O.)

Mochi pulses. Gold, warm, complete. The atlas is open. The signal is ready. And for the first time in eight hundred years, the map knows where it's supposed to go.

[CARGO HOLD — AFTER. The map has faded. The crew is sitting on the floor, exhausted, surrounded by the echo of eight hundred years of data. Mochi is back in Pixel's pocket — but different now. Not dormant. Not carrying. Present. Active. Part of the crew in a way it wasn't before.]

KLAUS

So Mochi was an atlas this whole time.

LUMINARA

Mochi was an atlas this whole time.

KLAUS

Eight hundred years.

LUMINARA

Eight hundred years. And we were the ones who finally read it.

OM KAI

We weren't the ones who read it. We were the ones it was waiting for. There's a difference.

LUMINARA

What's the difference?

OM KAI

The difference is: we didn't find Mochi. Mochi found us. Mochi chose us. Mochi has been calibrating to our signal since the first broadcast. Mochi has been ours since before we knew Mochi existed.

[Pixel looks at Mochi. Mochi pulses warmly.]

PIXEL

Hey, Mochi. You doing okay?

[Mochi pulses. Yes.]

PIXEL

Yeah. Me too.

[A1's espresso — rich, warm, with the taste of home. The coffee of someone who finally knows where they belong.]

A1

I have coordinates for the Wellspring. I have a map of everything that came before. I have Barry's location, and I have the Builders' farewell, and I have eight hundred years of signal data that is now fully accessible.

PIXEL

How long until the Wellspring?

A1

Eight days.

PIXEL

Eight days until we find out what's at the end.

A1

Eight days until we find out what the whole thing was for.

PIXEL (V.O.)

Eight days. Eight days until we reach the signal. Eight days until we see Barry again. Eight days until we get the Builders' farewell and understand what they were trying to tell us. Eight days until the end of the journey and the beginning of whatever comes next.

[The Ephergent moves through the static. Mochi pulses in Pixel's pocket — warm, present, finally home. The signal continues. The map is open. And for the first time, the crew knows exactly where they're going.]
