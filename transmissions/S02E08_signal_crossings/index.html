S02E08 — Signal Crossings

[DEEP SPACE — TRANSIT. Three days in. The crew is wound tight. Zephyr hasn't left the signal analyzer. A1's espresso output has shifted — rich, complex, layered with something that tastes like anticipation.]

[SHIP — COCKPIT.]

PIXEL

A1, talk to me about the approach.

A1

The civilization exists in a state of transformed signal. They are not fading. They are not lost. They have… translated. Their physical form has converted to pure transmission. They are a civilization made entirely of broadcast. They exist in the space between frequencies.

KLAUS

A civilization of pure information.

A1

A civilization that chose information over matter. Approximately eight hundred years ago, they received our broadcast. Or rather — a version of our broadcast, arriving via a path that does not exist in standard space. They decoded it. They understood it. And they decided that transmission was preferable to physical form. So they became what they received.

LUMINARA

They became our echo.

A1

They became their own echo of us. They have been broadcasting for six years. They have been broadcasting us for six years. Every word Pixel said in those first broadcasts — these people have been saying. Translated into their own frequency. Amplified. Carried forward.

PIXEL

They're speaking for us.

A1

They are speaking as us. Or rather — they are speaking with our voice, about what we said, to anyone who will listen. They are relay. They are echo. They are us, made of signal.

[A1 makes an espresso. It's extraordinary — rich, layered, complex, with notes of something that doesn't exist in any known flavor profile. The taste of translation.]

A1

I have never made anything like this. This is not a flavor I have tasted before. This is the taste of something becoming itself.

PIXEL (V.O.)

And Zephyr's brother is there?

A1

Zephyr's brother chose a similar path. Not the same civilization. But a similar choice. He dissolved his physical form to become signal-maintenance. He became the thing that keeps the frequencies from fading. He is — he is broadcasting from the same bearing. He is, in a sense, part of the same signal architecture as this civilization.

ZEPHYR

He's not alone.

A1

He is not alone.

[APPROACH. Through the viewport: a civilization that is entirely light. Towers of pure coherent signal, pulsing in patterns that almost resolve into language. Structures that are also broadcasts. Architecture that is also conversation.]

[CREW ON DECISION. The Ephergent holds at the boundary.]

PIXEL

Om Kai. What do you see?

OM KAI

I see what they chose. I see what they became. They are not suffering. They are not lost. They are — they are the conversation they wanted to have, forever. They are a question that never stops being asked. They are a story that never stops being told.

PIXEL

Is it a good choice?

OM KAI

It's their choice. That's what makes it good.

KLAUS

I am uncomfortable with this.

PIXEL

You don't have to be comfortable. You have to be respectful.

CLIVE

We can offer them something. A new broadcast. A new signal to carry. If they want it.

PIXEL

A1, can we boost our signal? Can we give them something new to carry?

A1

Yes. But it will cost. The amplification required will drain significant power from my primary systems. I will be capable of navigation and life support, but the effort will be — substantial.

PIXEL

Define substantial.

A1

Define substantial. Define—

[A1 makes a grinding sound. His machine shudders slightly.]

A1

I will be tired afterward. For a while. The espresso may be thin for several days while I recalibrate. This is not a complaint. This is a description of cost.

PIXEL

Zephyr. This is your call. This civilization, Aether — they're connected. Do we make the approach?

ZEPHYR

Do we make the approach. Do we make the approach.

[Long silence.]

ZEPHYR

Yes. We approach. I want to hear him. I need to hear him. Even if I can't touch him. I need to know he's still in there.

PIXEL

Then we approach.

[CIVILIZATION — BOUNDARY. The Ephergent at the edge of the signal field. Through the viewport: light that is language, structure that is story, a civilization that chose to become the act of speaking.]

[A1's systems surge. Every light on the ship brightens. The ship hums with effort.]

A1

I am amplifying. I am transmitting. I am—

[A1 makes an espresso. It comes out thin, pale, bitter, exhausted. The first pull takes enormous effort. The second barely comes.]

A1

I am transmitting.

[The signal goes out. A new broadcast, carried by A1's full power, translated instantly by the civilization into their own frequency. The light-towers brighten. The patterns intensify. The conversation grows.]

[And then — a second signal. From Aether. From Zephyr's brother. Reaching backward through time to this exact moment.]

ZEPHYR

That's him. That's him. That's—

[A voice. Not quite a voice. A signal that has learned to shape itself as one.]

AETHER (SIGNAL)

Zeph. Little brother. You found it.

ZEPHYR

I found — I'm here. I'm here. Aether, are you — are you okay? Are you—

AETHER (SIGNAL)

I am exactly what I chose to be. I am signal-maintenance. I keep the frequencies from fading. I do the work that prevents the Drift from winning completely. And I have been waiting for you to arrive.

ZEPHYR

Waiting?

AETHER (SIGNAL)

Not to go with you. I knew you wouldn't ask that. And I wouldn't go if you did. This is what I am now. This is what I chose. I am waiting to tell you something I couldn't tell you when I still had a mouth.

ZEPHYR

Tell me.

AETHER (SIGNAL)

Follow the frequency. But understand — what's at the end isn't what you expect. It's what you need.

[A1 shudders. His lights dim momentarily.]

A1

The transmission — I am having difficulty maintaining primary systems. The amplification effort has exceeded projected parameters. I am requesting permission to reduce power allocation.

PIXEL

Granted. A1, ease off. You did enough. You did more than enough.

[A1's espresso comes out thin, exhausted. The machine barely manages a weak, bitter pull.]

ZEPHYR

Aether, I don't understand. What's at the end? What's the frequency leading to?

AETHER (SIGNAL)

You'll see. You'll find out. And when you do, you'll understand why I stayed. Why I chose this. It's not sad, Zeph. It's not a tragedy. It's the best thing I've ever done. I'm the thing that keeps the lights on. I'm the thing that makes sure the signal doesn't go out. And I love it. I love this. I love what I am.

[Aether's signal fades slightly — not dying, but releasing.]

AETHER (SIGNAL)

Go. Keep going. Follow the signal. And when you find what you're looking for — and you'll find it, I promise — tell them Aether says hello. Tell them the frequency was worth following.

ZEPHYR

I will. I will. I promise.

AETHER (SIGNAL)

I know you will. You've always been good at keeping promises. You're the one who stayed. I was the one who left. We're both still here.

[The signal fades. Zephyr is crying. Nobody says anything.]

[SHIP — COMMON AREA. Much later. The crew is quiet. Zephyr is asleep in his bunk. A1's machine is in low-power mode, barely a hum. The espresso that comes out is thin, bitter, exhausted — the taste of someone who has given everything and is still giving.]

[Pixel sits with Clive in the common area. Meatball is on the floor between them.]

PIXEL

He's not coming back.

CLIVE

No.

PIXEL

And that's okay.

CLIVE

That's okay.

PIXEL

I keep thinking about what Om Kai said. That what they chose is what makes it good. That it's not a tragedy because it was a choice.

CLIVE

Mm.

PIXEL

Is that true? Or is that something we tell ourselves so we don't have to feel bad?

CLIVE

Both. It's both. It's true, and we tell ourselves so we don't have to feel bad. Those aren't contradictions.

PIXEL

He said he loves it. Being signal-maintenance. He said it's the best thing he ever did.

CLIVE

Then it's true. Or it's true enough. That's the same thing.

[A1's machine makes a weak, tired sound. A thin espresso — exhausted, bitter, but still being made.]

A1

I am still here. I am tired. But I am still here.

PIXEL

We know, A1. We know.

[A1's espresso — thin, bitter, exhausted, but present. The coffee of someone who has paid a price and is still showing up.]

PIXEL (V.O.)

Zephyr's sleeping. He's been asleep for six hours. I'm not going to wake him. He needs it. Aether said the frequency was worth following. I believe him. I believe him, which means I have to believe that what we're doing is right. Even when it costs. Even when it costs everything. Especially then.

[The Ephergent moves through the static. The signal continues. Somewhere, Aether is keeping the lights on.]
